The Kabardian language is a member of the Abhkaz-Adyghean (Northwest Caucasian) language family. With Ubykh being recently extinct, it constitutes one branch of this family together with the closely related Adyghean language, while Abhkaz and Abaza constitute the other branch:

Abhkaz-Adyghean language family
Abkhaz-Abaza branch Adyghean-Kabardian branch
  1. Abkhaz
  2. Abaza
  1. Ubykh (extinct)
  2. Adyghean
  3. Kabardian

The frequent common name for Adygheans and Kabardians is Circassians.
